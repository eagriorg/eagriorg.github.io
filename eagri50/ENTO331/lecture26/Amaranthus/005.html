PESTS OF AMARANTHUS :: MINOR PESTS :: TORTOISE BEETLE

 

 

3. Tortoise beetle: Aspidomorpha exilis (Cassididae: Coleoptera)

Eggs are laid singly on ventral surface of leaves. Grubs and adults feed by scrapping outer tissues of leaves. Pupation takes place on leaf surface. Life cycle is completed in 15 to 30 days.
