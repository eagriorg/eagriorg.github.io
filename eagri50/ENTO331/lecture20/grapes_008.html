8.  Plume moth: Oxyptilus regulus (Pterophoridae: Lepidoptera)
        
Plume moth lays eggs singly around fruit stalks, on peduncles and wherever grapes in a bunch are contiguous. Larva enters grapes near fruit stalk and tunnels through pulp into seeds. A single larva attacks many grapes.
